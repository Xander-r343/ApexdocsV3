# Strafe Tuner

The Strafe Tuner calibrates the **strafe (lateral) controller** — the PDFL loop responsible for side-to-side translational motion. Proper tuning ensures accurate lateral distance tracking and minimizes overshoot during strafing movements.

<Callout type="warning">
  Clear a large open area before running this tuner. The robot will strafe laterally during the tuning sequence.
</Callout>

Select the **Strafe Tuner** OpMode on your Driver Station and follow the on-screen telemetry prompts to begin.

---

### Tuning Procedure

- Observe the robot's lateral response during each strafe movement — look for overshoot, oscillation, or sluggishness.
- Adjust the following parameters in **Panels** based on observed behavior:
  - **Proportional Gain (P)** — Controls how aggressively the robot corrects lateral position error. Increase to reduce steady-state error; too high causes oscillation.
  - **Derivative Gain (D)** — Dampens the correction response. Increase to reduce overshoot and oscillation.
  - **Minimum Power (F)** — Sets a feedforward floor to overcome static friction at low error values.
  - **Deadzone (L)** — Defines the error threshold below which no correction is applied. Helps prevent jitter near the target.
- Repeat the strafe sequence and re-tune until motion is smooth, accurate, and settled.

---

### Applying Your Values

Once tuning is complete, update the following line in your robot configuration with the values obtained:

```java
.setStrafeCoeffs(new PDFLCoefficients(p, d, f, l))
```

<Callout type="info">
  Replace `p`, `d`, `f`, and `l` with the final tuned values shown in telemetry. You may also adjust the tolerances after tuning to fine-tune settling behavior.
</Callout>
